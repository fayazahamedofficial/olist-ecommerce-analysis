## Executive Summary

Analysis of 110,832 order items from the Olist Brazilian 
E-Commerce platform (2016-2018) reveals that 6.58% of orders 
are delivered late and 14.68% of customers leave complaint 
reviews (1-2 stars). Late delivery rates increased from 5.53% 
in 2017 to 7.48% in 2018, indicating a worsening trend. 
An estimated R$423,000 in revenue is currently at risk across 
the top five problem categories. Immediate action on three 
identified root causes could significantly reduce late 
deliveries and improve customer satisfaction.

## Root Cause Analysis

Three root causes were identified through data analysis:

### 1. Product Weight
Late orders are on average 20% heavier than on-time orders
(2,465g vs 2,061g). Heavier products require special handling
and are more likely to be delayed in transit.

### 2. Seller Performance
One seller alone has a 94.46% late delivery rate across 469
orders. The top 5 underperforming sellers all have late rates
above 35% — indicating seller-specific operational failures
rather than platform-wide logistics issues.

### 3. Regional Logistics
Northeast Brazilian states consistently show the highest late
delivery rates:

| State | Late Rate |
|---|---|
| Alagoas (AL) | 21% |
| Maranhão (MA) | 18% |
| Sergipe (SE) | 16% |
| Ceará (CE) | 14% |
| Piauí (PI) | 14% |

These states are geographically distant from the main
distribution centers in Southeast Brazil, suggesting a
logistics infrastructure gap.

## Recommendations

### Priority 1 — Immediate (0-30 days)
**Audit and retrain high-risk sellers**
- Suspend or issue performance warning to seller with 94% 
  late rate (469 orders affected)
- Review top 5 underperforming sellers — all above 35% 
  late rate
- Implement seller performance dashboard with weekly 
  late rate monitoring
- Expected impact: 15-20% reduction in overall late 
  delivery rate

### Priority 2 — Short Term (30-90 days)
**Improve Northeast Brazil logistics**
- Partner with regional logistics providers in AL, MA, 
  SE, CE, PI
- Set up regional distribution points in Northeast Brazil
- Offer extended delivery estimates for Northeast orders 
  to manage customer expectations
- Expected impact: Reduce Northeast late rate from 
  21% to under 10%

### Priority 3 — Medium Term (90-180 days)
**Implement weight-based handling policy**
- Flag products over 2,500g for special handling
- Partner with freight companies specializing in heavy 
  goods
- Adjust delivery time estimates for heavy products
- Expected impact: Reduce late rate for heavy products 
  by 25-30%

### Priority 4 — Ongoing
**Monitor Revenue at Risk KPI monthly**
- Track R$423k revenue at risk across top 5 categories
- Set target: reduce revenue at risk by 50% within 
  12 months
- Review complaint rates monthly — target below 10% 
  for all categories
